Hyperbolic Lattice Topology
Temporal Coherence
Quantum Temporal Coherence is the foundation of QTCL's consensus mechanism. By maintaining quantum coherence across distributed validators, we achieve Byzantine agreement without sacrificing quantum properties. The temporal dimension allows classical and quantum proofs to coexist.
NIST Post-Quantum Standard
QTCL implements FIPS 203/204/205 compliant lattice-based cryptography. Module-Learning-With-Errors (ML-LWE) provides quantum-resistant security for wallets, signatures, and oracle commitments.
Wallet Architecture
Wallets use lattice-based key pairs derived from post-quantum random sources. HD wallet derivation follows quantum-resistant paths, ensuring wallet hierarchies remain secure against quantum adversaries.
Blockchain Consensus
QTCL combines Hybrid PoW + BFT consensus. Hyperbolic lattice puzzles replace traditional hashing, while W-state entanglement provides quantum consensus witnesses that prove validator agreement.
Quantum Entropy Mining
Miners perform entropy measurement on recovered W-states to derive puzzle seeds. This couples quantum information directly into the consensus mechanism, making QTCL the first blockchain secured by quantum entanglement.
